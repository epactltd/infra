## 1. Purpose

Define acceptable and unacceptable use of EPACT LTD's information systems, networks, and data.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all users:
- EPACT employees (all roles)
- Contractors and consultants
- Temporary staff
- Third parties with system access

Covers all systems:
- Computers (laptops, workstations, servers)
- Mobile devices (phones, tablets)
- Network (WiFi, VPN, internet)
- AWS infrastructure
- Applications and databases
- Email and communication tools

---

## 3. Acceptable Use

### 3.1 Business Use (Primary)
**Systems provided for**:
- Performing job duties
- Business communication
- Professional development related to role
- Authorized company purposes

### 3.2 Personal Use (Limited)
**Reasonable personal use** permitted:
- Brief personal email (lunch break, before/after work hours)
- Personal phone calls (reasonable, not excessive)
- Personal browsing (news, shopping) during breaks

**Limits**:
- Does not interfere with work duties
- Does not consume significant bandwidth or resources
- Not during billable client time
- Complies with all other policy provisions (no illegal activity)

### 3.3 Monitoring and Privacy
**Users informed and acknowledge**:
- EPACT monitors system usage for security and compliance
- No expectation of privacy for company systems
- Personal use subject to monitoring (emails, web history, etc.)
- Monitoring for security, not surveillance
- Personal accounts (personal email, social media) on personal devices recommended for privacy

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

### 4.1 Illegal Activities (Zero Tolerance)
❌ **Strictly Prohibited**:
- Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content (child abuse imagery, terrorist material, etc.)
- Hacking, cracking, or unauthorized access attempts
- Copyright infringement (pirated software, movies, music)
- Fraud or financial crimes
- Violating export controls or sanctions
- Identity theft or impersonation
- Harassment, stalking, or threats

**Consequences**: Immediate termination, referral to law enforcement

### 4.2 Security Violations (Subject to Disciplinary Action)
❌ **Prohibited**:
- Sharing passwords or credentials
- Disabling security controls (antivirus, firewall, encryption)
- Bypassing access controls or authentication
- Installing unauthorized software
- Connecting unauthorized devices to company network
- Removing data without authorization
- Clicking suspicious links or opening malware (after training)
- Social engineering (attempting to trick colleagues)

**Consequences**: Verbal/written warning (first offense); termination (repeat or severe)

### 4.3 Misuse of Resources (Disciplinary Action)
❌ **Prohibited**:
- Excessive personal use (>1 hour per day cumulative)
- Personal commercial activities or side businesses
- Cryptocurrency mining on company systems
- Torrenting or large downloads (non-work related)
- Streaming video/audio for entertainment during work hours
- Using company resources for personal profit
- Game playing (except team-building approved activities)

**Consequences**: Verbal warning (first); written warning (repeat); termination (severe)

### 4.4 Inappropriate Content (Disciplinary Action)
❌ **Prohibited**:
- Pornography or sexually explicit material
- Hate speech, discriminatory, or offensive content
- Violent or disturbing content
- Gambling (online betting, poker during work)
- Content harassing colleagues or others

**Consequences**: Written warning (first); termination (severe or repeat)

### 4.5 Unauthorized Disclosure (Severe - Termination)
❌ **Prohibited**:
- Sharing confidential information with unauthorized parties
- Public disclosure of security vulnerabilities (without approval)
- Social media posts about confidential projects
- Discussing customer data in public or on personal social media
- Leaking business strategies or financial information

**Consequences**: Immediate termination; potential legal action; regulatory notification (GDPR breach)

---

## 5. Email Use

### 5.1 Business Email
- **Professional communication**: Appropriate language and tone
- **Company email only**: No personal email for business communications
- **Signature**: Include full name, position, company contact details
- **Subject line**: Descriptive
- **Reply-all**: Use judiciously (avoid email storms)

### 5.2 Email Security
- **Phishing awareness**: Do not click suspicious links; verify sender
- **Attachments**: Scan with antivirus before opening
- **Sensitive data**: Encrypted attachments for CONFIDENTIAL; no RESTRICTED via email
- **External email**: Use caution; verify recipient before sending confidential info
- **Auto-forward**: Not permitted (risk of data leakage)
- **BCC for mass emails**: Respect recipient privacy

### 5.3 Prohibited Email Use
- Spam or chain letters
- Personal mass mailings
- Harassing or offensive emails
- Fraudulent or deceptive emails (phishing, impersonation)
- Auto-responders revealing confidential information

---

## 6. Internet and Web Use

### 6.1 Acceptable Web Browsing
- **Work-related**: Research, tools, documentation, professional development
- **Reasonable personal**: News, banking during breaks
- **Social media**: Professional use (LinkedIn, company accounts); limited personal during breaks

### 6.2 Prohibited Web Activities
- Sites containing illegal content
- Sites with malware or phishing risks (blocked by filtering)
- Excessive streaming (video, music) during work hours
- Peer-to-peer file sharing (BitTorrent)
- Circumventing web filters or proxies
- Anonymous browsing tools (for bypassing controls; Tor prohibited except security research)

### 6.3 Web Filtering
- **Malicious sites**: Blocked automatically
- **Inappropriate content**: Blocked (adult, gambling, hate, violence)
- **Social media**: Allowed (within acceptable use limits)
- **Override requests**: Case-by-case approval by ISMS Lead (legitimate business need)

---

## 7. Software Installation

### 7.1 Approved Software
- **Pre-installed**: Company-approved software on laptops
- **Self-service**: From approved software portal/catalog
- **Open-source tools**: With approval (license compliance check)

### 7.2 Prohibited Software
- Unlicensed or pirated software
- Peer-to-peer file sharing (BitTorrent, LimeWire)
- Remote access tools (TeamViewer, AnyDesk) unless approved
- Cryptocurrency miners
- Hacking tools (unless security team role and approved)
- Software from untrusted sources

### 7.3 Approval Process
**New software request**:
1. Business justification submitted to line manager
2. Security assessment (ISMS Lead)
3. License compliance check
4. Malware scan
5. Approval or denial with reasoning
6. If approved: Installation by IT or self-service
7. Added to approved software list

## 8. Social Media

### 8.1 Personal Social Media
- **Separate personal from professional**: Personal views do not represent EPACT
- **No confidential information**: Company projects, customers, financials
- **Respect colleagues**: No disparaging comments about company or colleagues
- **During work**: Minimal personal social media during work hours

### 8.2 Professional Social Media (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- **Identify affiliation**: "I work for EPACT" (optional)
- **Professional content**: Share industry insights, company public announcements
- **Company approval**: For speaking on behalf of company
- **Confidential information**: Never share without explicit approval

### 8.3 Company Social Media Accounts
- **Authorized users only**: Business Development Director, designated marketing personnel
- **Content calendar**: Planned and approved content
- **No ad-hoc posts**: All posts approved by Managing Director
- **Respond professionally**: To comments and messages
- **Security**: Strong passwords, MFA enabled, separate company account

## 10. Data Protection

### 10.1 Customer and Personal Data
- **Accessed only** for authorized business purposes
- **Minimum necessary**: Access only data needed for task
- **No unauthorized disclosure**: Sharing with unauthorized parties prohibited
- **Secure handling**: Per Data Protection Policy
- **Data exports**: Require approval; logged and audited

### 10.2 Confidential Business Data
- **Protect**: Encrypt, access control, proper classification
- **No unauthorized copying**: To personal devices or external media
- **Disposal**: Secure deletion, shredding

## 11. Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

### 11.1 Limited BYOD
**Allowed on personal devices** (limited scope):
- Email via webmail (no local client)
- MFA apps (Google Authenticator, Authy)
- Messaging (Slack mobile app)
- Calendar access

**Prohibited on Personal Devices**:
- VPN or production system access
- Downloading/storing RESTRICTED data
- Code development or deployment
- Administrative functions

### 11.2 BYOD Security Requirements
**If BYOD permitted** (explicit approval required):
- Device encryption enabled (iOS, Android)
- Screen lock (password/biometric)
- OS and apps up to date
- Antivirus (if applicable)
- No jailbreak/root
- Company data segregated (containerization if MDM available)
- Remote wipe consent (company data only)

### 11.3 Lost/Stolen Personal Device
- Report to ISMS Lead immediately
- EPACT remotely wipes company data (if MDM capability)
- Password resets for accounts accessed from device
- Police report recommended

## 12. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 12.1 System Monitoring
**EPACT monitors** (users informed and acknowledge):
- Internet usage (URLs visited, bandwidth)
- Email (subject lines, metadata; content only if security concern)
- File transfers (uploads/downloads)
- Application usage
- System logs (authentications, errors, admin actions)

**Purpose**:
- Security incident detection
- Policy compliance verification
- Investigating suspected violations
- Performance and capacity planning

**Privacy**:
- Monitoring proportionate to purpose
- Personal privacy respected where possible
- No covert surveillance
- HR consulted for employee monitoring issues

### 12.2 Investigations
**Triggered by**:
- Automated alerts (GuardDuty, CloudWatch alarms)
- Manager request (suspected policy violation)
- Security incident
- Customer complaint
- Regulatory inquiry

**Process**:
- Authorized by ISMS Lead or Managing Director
- Fair and confidential
- Evidence collected (logs, screenshots)
- Employee opportunity to respond
- Findings documented
- Disciplinary action if violation confirmed

## 13. Consequences of Violations

### 13.1 Disciplinary Actions
| **Violation Type** | **First Offense** | **Repeat Offense** |
|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Minor** (e.g., excessive personal use) | Verbal warning | Written warning |
| **Moderate** (e.g., unapproved software) | Written warning | Final written warning |
| **Serious** (e.g., sharing passwords) | Final warning or suspension | Termination |
| **Gross misconduct** (e.g., illegal activity, data theft) | Immediate termination | N/A |

### 13.2 Additional Consequences
- Revocation of system privileges
- Demotion or transfer
- Legal action (criminal or civil)
- Regulatory reporting (data breaches)
- Financial liability (damages, breach costs)

### 13.3 Mitigating Factors
- No prior violations
- Self-reported violation
- Genuine mistake (after training)
- Cooperation with investigation

**Aggravating Factors**:
- Repeat violations
- Attempted concealment
- Deliberate or malicious
- Customer or business harm resulted

## 14. User Responsibilities

### 14.1 Every User Must
✅ **Do**:
- Use strong, unique passwords (14+ characters)
- Enable MFA on all accounts
- Lock screen when leaving device
- Report security incidents and suspicious activity
- Complete annual security training
- Protect confidential information
- Use company systems only for authorized purposes
- Comply with all information security policies
- Ask if unsure (better to ask than violate policy)

### 14.2 Every User Must Not
❌ **Don't**:
- Share your password or access credentials
- Use company systems for illegal activities
- Bypass security controls
- Install unauthorized software
- Store company data on personal devices (except limited BYOD)
- Disclose confidential information externally
- Click suspicious links or open unexpected attachments
- Ignore security warnings or certificate errors
